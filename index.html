Part 3- Exterminating the flies

The aim of part 3 is to produce an alternate method of exterminating flies rather than just using fly spray which may prove inefficient as a from of exterminating flies. The method derived as a substitute for fly spray was deploying a Parsonsâ Chameleon in the centre of the room. This is seen as an effective method as the chameleon has specific range in which the flies enter, they will be eaten.

The Parsonsâ Chameleon is the biggest species of chameleon and predator of flies. The chameleon has an average length of approximately 60cm. The chameleons tongue, the biggest among reptiles, mammals, and birds relative to body size, can range from one to two times its body length. In this investigation the tongue of the parsons chameleon is going to 1.5 times is body length of 60cm, therefore the range of the tongue is 90cm. The Parsonsâ Chameleon is also known for its âballisticâ speed when hunting prey, its tongue can reach a speed of 27 metres per second, if the speed of the tongue is kept constant it means the tongue can reach 90cm of range in approximately 0.033 seconds. With this chameleons range and speed, it presents itself as a highly efficient fly hunter. The model for the chameleon will be based on its range of attack, 90cm, this will be presented by a sphere alike to that in part 1.
